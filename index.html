Meet Sera

The person behind every groom, every belly rub, every safe send-home.

Picture Pawfect started the way the best things do — with love. I already had a basic knowledge of grooming my own dogs, and I've always had a deep love for animals. After 11 years of grooming (4 professionally), I've turned that love into my dream come true.

I'm gentle, caring, and considerate with every dog I work on. What makes me different? I know when a dog has had enough and it's time to stop — regardless of whether the groom is finished. Your dog's comfort is always my top priority, not the money.

I once took on a dog that other groomers called "vicious." They warned me how hard she was to groom. But she gave me no problem. I looked into her eyes and she looked into mine, and she trusted me. From that moment, I make it a point to build a special bond with every one of my little fur friends. They have souls too.

I always say, "loving animals is a blessing and a curse." It's a blessing because there's nothing like the bond you share with them. But when they're sick or you lose one and you can't change that — that's the curse. That love is what drives everything I do here.

Your First Visit

What to expect when your dog visits me.

I know it's not easy leaving your baby somewhere new. Here's what happens so you can feel completely comfortable.

Before the Visit

  • We'll chat about your dog's personality and any past grooming experience
  • Let me know about any anxiety, sensitivities, or health concerns
  • Share any behavior notes so I can give them the best experience
  • Make sure your dog's vaccinations are up to date

During the Visit

  • Your dog gets my full, one-on-one attention — no other dogs being worked on at the same time
  • I go at their pace, not mine, and take breaks when needed
  • If they're stressed or overwhelmed, I stop — even if the groom isn't finished
  • Calm music, gentle hands, and a whole lot of patience

Have an anxious dog? That's okay. Some of my best bonds have been with dogs that other groomers struggled with. I don't force anything. I take my time, build trust, and let your dog tell me what they're comfortable with. I'd rather me do it with patience than someone else push through.

Policies

A few things to know before we get started.

These help everything run smoothly for you, your dog, and for me. I appreciate your understanding.

♥ Cancellations

Please give notice if you need to cancel or reschedule. Last-minute cancellations affect my schedule and other clients waiting for spots.

♥ Drop-Off & Pick-Up

Please be on time for drop-off and pick-up. Being timely helps everything run smoothly for everyone.

♥ Vaccinations

Please make sure your dog's vaccinations are up to date. This keeps all the dogs in my care safe and healthy.

♥ Behavior & Temperament

Let me know about any behavioral concerns upfront. I can work with it — I just need to know so I can give your dog the best experience.

♥ Matting & Coat Condition

Heavily matted coats may require a shorter cut for your dog's comfort. I'll always talk to you about it first.

♥ Senior Dogs

Age isn't a deal breaker, but we need to have a heartfelt conversation first about any risks involved. Your dog's safety always comes first.

FAQ

Questions I hear most.

If you're thinking about bringing your baby here, these might help.

How do I request a spot?

Text me! That's the best way to reach me. I don't answer unknown numbers due to spam, so texting is the way to go. You can also call and leave a voicemail. I usually respond within the hour.

What if my dog is anxious or nervous?

That's completely okay. I take my time, go at their pace, and build trust before I do anything. Some of my strongest bonds have been with dogs other groomers struggled with. Patience changes everything.

Why does pricing vary?

Every dog is different. Breed, size, coat condition, behavior, and the services your dog needs all play a role. I'll let you know up front — no surprises.

Is my dog in a cage all day?

No. This isn't a big commercial operation with rows of kennels. Your dog gets personal attention in a real home environment where they're comfortable and cared for.

What should I know before boarding?

Any care notes, routines, behavior details, and health info should be shared when you reach out. The more I know, the better I can care for your baby.

Do you accept new clients?

Yes, when I have availability. Reaching out first helps me make sure we're a good fit for each other.

What info do you need from new clients?

Any past grooming experience your dog has had (if any), and anything about their behavior or temperament. That's it to get started.

Do you only groom dogs?

Yes, I only work on dogs. But I love all breeds, all sizes, and all personalities. Every dog is welcome here.
